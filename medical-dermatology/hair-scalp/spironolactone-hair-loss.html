Spironolactone for Hair Loss: Antiandrogen Therapy

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Overview

Spironolactone is an oral potassium-sparing diuretic with antiandrogen properties used off-label for female pattern hair loss and other androgen-dependent alopecias in women. While not FDA-approved specifically for hair loss, spironolactone effectively reduces circulating androgens and blocks androgen receptor signaling, making it particularly valuable for women with hyperandrogenism or those intolerant of other treatments.

Mechanism of Action

Spironolactone functions as a non-selective androgen receptor antagonist and inhibits ovarian and adrenal androgen synthesis. By blocking DHT effects at the hair follicle receptor level, it prevents androgen-induced miniaturization. This dual action makes spironolactone effective even when DHT levels are not significantly elevated.

Dosing and Administration

Hair loss treatment typically employs 100-200 mg daily in divided doses. Most patients receive 50-100 mg twice daily. Therapeutic effects require 3-6 months to become apparent, with maximum benefit at 12 months. Combination with oral contraceptives or other hormonal therapy enhances efficacy and provides contraceptive benefit.

Efficacy in Female Pattern Hair Loss

Clinical experience demonstrates effectiveness in slowing hair loss and promoting regrowth in 50-75% of women, particularly those with documented hyperandrogenism or PCOS. Response rates appear superior in younger women with higher baseline androgens. Efficacy comparable to or exceeding minoxidil has been reported in some series.

Adverse Effects

Common side effects include irregular menstrual cycles, breast tenderness, decreased libido, and fatigue. Hyperkalemia represents a significant concern, particularly in patients with renal impairment or concurrent ACE inhibitor/ARB use. Regular monitoring of serum potassium and renal function is essential.

Monitoring Requirements

Baseline potassium, creatinine, and blood pressure assessment should be obtained. Periodic monitoring of potassium and renal function (every 3-6 months initially, then annually) is essential. Contraindications include pregnancy, renal failure, and uncontrolled hyperkalemia. Drug interactions with other agents affecting potassium homeostasis require careful consideration.

Contraindications in Pregnancy

Spironolactone is contraindicated in pregnancy due to potential feminization of male fetuses. Reliable contraception is essential for women of childbearing potential. This limitation precludes use in premenopausal women not using contraception.

Combination Therapy

Combined spironolactone and minoxidil or finasteride provides superior outcomes compared to monotherapy in many patients. Oral contraceptives combined with spironolactone offer both hair loss treatment and contraceptive benefits.
