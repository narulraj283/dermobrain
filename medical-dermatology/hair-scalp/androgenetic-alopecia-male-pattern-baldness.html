Androgenetic Alopecia: Male Pattern Baldness

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Overview

Androgenetic alopecia (AGA), commonly known as male pattern baldness, is the most prevalent form of hair loss in men, affecting approximately 50% of males by age 50. This progressive condition results from a combination of genetic predisposition and sensitivity to dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone derived from testosterone.

Pathophysiology

In genetically susceptible individuals, hair follicles in the scalp contain increased numbers of androgen receptors. These follicles undergo miniaturization when exposed to DHT, a process wherein the hair shaft becomes progressively thinner and shorter. The growth phase (anagen) shortens while the resting phase (telogen) lengthens, resulting in visible hair loss.

Clinical Presentation

Male pattern baldness typically presents with:

  • Receding hairline (bitemporal recession)
  • Crown thinning or balding
  • Progressive loss over months to years
  • Preserved occipital and temporal hair
  • No associated scalp inflammation or symptoms

Diagnosis

Diagnosis is primarily clinical, based on hair loss pattern and distribution. The Norwood-Hamilton scale categorizes severity from Type I (minimal recession) to Type VII (extensive baldness). Dermoscopy may reveal decreased hair density and hair shaft diameter variation. Scalp biopsy is rarely necessary unless atypical features are present.

Treatment Options

Topical minoxidil and oral finasteride are FDA-approved treatments that can slow progression and promote regrowth. Hair transplantation offers definitive restoration, while low-level laser therapy and platelet-rich plasma treatments show promise in preliminary studies. Early intervention yields superior outcomes.

Prognosis

Without treatment, androgenetic alopecia typically progresses slowly. Approximately 25% of men experience noticeable hair loss by age 30, and this increases with advancing age. Early recognition and treatment initiation provide the best opportunity for hair preservation.
