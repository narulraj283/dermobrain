Understanding Alopecia Areata: Recognition, Diagnosis, and Care

By DermoBrain Medical Editorial Team Updated 2026-03-03 Medically Reviewed

Recognition of Understanding Alopecia Areata's full clinical spectrum improves diagnostic accuracy and therapeutic response.

Overview

First-line therapies for Understanding Alopecia Areata have established efficacy and favorable safety profiles.

  • Sudden onset of well-circumscribed alopecic patches
  • Non-scarring alopecia with preserved follicular openings
  • Exclamation mark hairs (fractured hairs with dark tips and pale roots) at patch margins
  • Nail pitting or ridging in 10-20% of cases
  • Possible beard, eyebrow, or body hair involvement
  • Pruritus or paresthesia preceding hair loss
