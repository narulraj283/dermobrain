Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis): Causes, Symptoms, and Treatment Options

By DermoBrain Medical Editorial Team Updated 2026-03-03 Medically Reviewed

Atopic dermatitis (eczema) is a chronic inflammatory skin condition characterized by intense itching, inflammation, and impaired skin barrier function.

Proper understanding of Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis) is essential for appropriate clinical management and patient outcomes.

Epidemiology and Clinical Significance

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis) demonstrates distinct epidemiological patterns across different demographic groups and geographic regions.

  • Factor 1: Genetic predisposition
  • Factor 2: Dysfunctional skin barrier
  • Factor 3: Immune system dysregulation
  • Factor 4: Filaggrin gene mutations
  • Factor 5: Environmental triggers
  • Factor 6: Often associated with asthma and allergic rhinitis

Causes and Risk Factors

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis) develops through dysregulation of normal skin homeostatic mechanisms.

  • Severe itching, often worse at night
  • Dry, sensitive, inflamed skin
  • Small raised bumps that may leak fluid when scratched
  • Swelling, cracking, and scaling
  • Raw, swollen skin from scratching

Pathophysiology and Disease Mechanisms

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis) develops through dysregulation of normal skin homeostatic mechanisms.

  • Emollients and moisturizers (ceramide-rich)
  • Topical corticosteroids
  • Topical calcineurin inhibitors
  • PDE4 inhibitors (crisaborole)
  • Systemic biologics (dupilumab, tralokinumab)
  • Phototherapy (UVB, PUVA)
  • Trigger avoidance
