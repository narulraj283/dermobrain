Understanding Razor Bumps (Pseudofolliculitis Barbae)

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

What are Razor Bumps?

Razor bumps, medically known as pseudofolliculitis barbae (PFB), are a common skin condition affecting men who shave regularly. This painful and unsightly condition occurs when hair follicles become irritated and inflamed after shaving.

Understanding the Mechanism

Unlike typical ingrown hairs, pseudofolliculitis barbae develops when shaved hair curls back and re-enters the skin near the follicle opening. This creates an inflammatory response that can become quite uncomfortable. The condition is particularly common in men with curly or coarse hair types.

Risk Factors

Several factors increase your risk of developing razor bumps. Men with deeply curved hair follicles are more susceptible. Shaving too closely to the skin, using dull razors, and shaving against the grain all contribute significantly. Your ethnicity can also play a role, as African American and Hispanic men experience higher rates.

Prevention Strategies

The most effective prevention involves proper shaving technique. Use a sharp razor and shave in the direction of hair growth rather than against it. Apply warm water and quality shaving cream before shaving to soften the hair. Consider shaving every other day instead of daily to allow skin recovery time.

Treatment Options

For mild cases, use gentle exfoliation and topical hydrocortisone cream. More severe cases may benefit from prescription treatments like tretinoin or benzoyl peroxide. Some men find success with bump-preventing products containing salicylic acid. In persistent cases, dermatologists may recommend laser hair removal as a permanent solution.

When to See a Dermatologist

If razor bumps don’t improve within two weeks or show signs of infection, schedule an appointment with a dermatologist. Professional treatments can provide faster relief and prevent scarring.
