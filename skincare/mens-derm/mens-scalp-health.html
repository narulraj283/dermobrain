Men's Dermatology

Men's Scalp Health: Beyond Hair Care

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Understanding Scalp Dermatology

Scalp health is foundational for hair health, yet many men neglect scalp care entirely. Common conditions like dandruff, seborrheic dermatitis, and scalp psoriasis significantly impact hair quality and appearance.

Dandruff vs. Seborrheic Dermatitis

Dandruff is mild flaking resulting from scalp dryness or oil imbalance. Seborrheic dermatitis is a chronic inflammatory condition causing more pronounced flaking, redness, and irritation. Malassezia yeast overgrowth contributes to both conditions. The distinction matters for treatment selection—dandruff improves with moisturizing while seborrheic dermatitis requires antifungal treatment.

Common Scalp Conditions

Psoriasis produces thick, silvery plaques on the scalp. Folliculitis affects scalp hair follicles similarly to beard folliculitis. Keratosis pilaris can occur on the scalp causing bumpy texture. Fungal infections like ringworm require immediate treatment to prevent spread. Allergic contact dermatitis develops from hair product reactions.

Proper Scalp Cleansing

Wash hair two to three times weekly with gentle shampoo formulated for your scalp type. Don’t use regular body soap, which is too harsh. Use lukewarm water, not hot water, which irritates the scalp. Massage gently for one minute to cleanse without traumatizing follicles. Rinse thoroughly to remove all product residue. Don’t shampoo every day—this strips natural oils and causes rebound oiliness.

Antifungal Treatments

For dandruff and seborrheic dermatitis, antifungal shampoos containing zinc pyrithione, ketoconazole, or selenium sulfide work effectively. Use two to three times weekly initially, then reduce frequency once symptoms improve. Rotate shampoos occasionally to prevent fungal resistance. Prescription-strength shampoos treat severe cases.

Scalp Moisturizing

Apply scalp oil or leave-in conditioner to dry, flaking areas. Coconut oil, jojoba oil, and argan oil nourish the scalp. Work the product into the scalp without getting hair greasy. Leave overnight or for a few hours before shampooing. Weekly deep conditioning treatments restore scalp health.

When to Seek Professional Help

If scalp conditions persist despite home treatment, cover large areas, or spread to the face or other body areas, consult a dermatologist. Severe cases may require prescription topical treatments, oral antifungals, or injections. Persistent scalp issues can eventually impact hair health, making professional evaluation worthwhile.
