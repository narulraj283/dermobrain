The Complete Sunscreen Application Guide

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Proper Sunscreen Application for Maximum Protection

Applying sunscreen correctly is just as important as choosing the right formula. Studies show that most people apply only 25-50% of the recommended amount, which significantly reduces the level of protection they receive. Learning the proper application technique ensures you're getting the full SPF benefit.

The Right Amount to Apply

The recommended amount for the entire face is approximately one quarter teaspoon or one nickel-sized amount. For the full body, use one ounce, which is roughly a shot glass full. These amounts might seem generous, but they're calibrated to match the sun protection factor testing standards. Using less than the recommended amount reduces protection exponentially.

When to Apply Sunscreen

Apply sunscreen as the last step in your morning skincare routine, after moisturizer but before makeup. Wait a few minutes for chemical sunscreens to absorb before applying other products. This prevents the sunscreen from being diluted. For mineral sunscreens, application can be more immediate. Never skip sunscreen under the assumption that your moisturizer or foundation contains SPF unless it's specifically formulated as a high-SPF product.

Application Technique

Dispense the product onto your fingertips or directly onto your face in five dots: forehead, nose, chin, and one cheek on each side. Gently spread and pat the product into the skin using upward motions, avoiding dragging motions that can disrupt other skincare products. Don't forget oft-missed areas like ears, the back of your neck, your hairline, and the tops of your feet if exposed.

Reapplication Best Practices

Reapply sunscreen every two hours, or immediately after swimming or heavy sweating. For makeup wearers, reapplication can be tricky. Use a powder sunscreen, sunscreen spray, or a hydrating mist with SPF as a convenient touch-up option. Alternatively, use a mineral powder with SPF over makeup to refresh protection throughout the day.

Combining Sunscreen with Other Products

Sunscreen works best when applied to clean, moisturized skin. If you use serums or treatments, apply them before sunscreen. Sunscreen should be applied before makeup but after all treatments. During evening routines, cleanse thoroughly to remove all sunscreen residue with a gentle cleanser or oil-based cleanser.
