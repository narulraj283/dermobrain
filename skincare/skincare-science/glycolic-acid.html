Glycolic Acid: The Alpha Hydroxy Acid Powerhouse

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Glycolic Acid: Harnessing the Power of AHAs

Glycolic acid is the most commonly used alpha hydroxy acid in skincare, known for its ability to gently exfoliate and transform skin texture. With the smallest molecular weight of all AHAs, glycolic acid penetrates effectively while remaining accessible to most skin types.

How Glycolic Acid Works

Glycolic acid exfoliates by breaking down the bonds between dead skin cells on the surface, promoting their removal. This increases cell turnover, revealing fresher, brighter skin beneath. Unlike physical exfoliants, chemical exfoliation with glycolic acid is gentle and doesn't cause microtears. The result is smoother texture, improved skin tone, and reduced appearance of fine lines and acne scars.

Glycolic Acid Concentrations and pH

Glycolic acid effectiveness depends on both concentration and pH. Professional chemical peels use 40-70% concentrations with very low pH, creating dramatic results. Over-the-counter products typically use 5-15% concentrations with higher pH, making them gentler for daily use. Lower pH products work more effectively as exfoliants, while higher pH products are milder but still beneficial.

Starting with Glycolic Acid

Beginners should start with lower concentrations, around 5-8%, applied 1-2 times per week. Allow the product to sit on skin for 5-10 minutes before rinsing with water, or leave on if it's a leave-on product. Gradually increase frequency to 3 times weekly as your skin adapts. Many people find 2-3 times weekly as their ideal maintenance frequency.

Benefits Beyond Exfoliation

Regular glycolic acid use improves overall skin quality by promoting collagen production and evening out skin tone. Pores appear smaller, texture is smoother, and dull skin becomes radiant. Those with acne-prone skin benefit from improved cell turnover that helps prevent congestion. Hyperpigmentation and post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation fade over time with consistent use.

Potential Side Effects and Considerations

Sensitive skin may experience irritation, redness, or stinging, particularly when first starting. Avoid combining glycolic acid with other exfoliating acids or strong actives initially. Glycolic acid increases sun sensitivity, making SPF 30+ absolutely essential. If your skin barrier is compromised, start with very low concentrations and short contact times.

Maximizing Results

Use glycolic acid in the evening when sun exposure is minimized. Follow with hydrating toner, serum, and moisturizer to support the skin barrier. Avoid using on sensitive or irritated skin. Once your skin tolerates regular glycolic acid, you can combine it with retinoids on alternate nights for comprehensive skin transformation.
