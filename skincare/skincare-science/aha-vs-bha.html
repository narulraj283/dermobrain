AHA vs BHA: Choosing the Right Chemical Exfoliant

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

AHA vs BHA: Understanding Chemical Exfoliation

Alpha hydroxy acids and beta hydroxy acids both exfoliate chemically, but through different mechanisms. Choosing between them depends on your skin type, concerns, and goals.

AHA: Water-Soluble Surface Exfoliants

Alpha hydroxy acids like glycolic acid, lactic acid, and mandelic acid are water-soluble, working on the skin surface. They break down bonds between dead skin cells, promoting shedding. AHAs increase cell turnover, brightening skin and improving texture. They provide gentle but effective exfoliation suitable for all skin types.

BHA: Oil-Soluble Pore-Penetrating Exfoliant

Salicylic acid is the primary BHA used in skincare. Being lipophilic, it dissolves in sebum and penetrates into pores. This makes BHA exceptionally effective for congestion, blackheads, and acne. BHA works within pores rather than just on the surface, addressing internal congestion.

When to Choose AHA

Use AHAs for general exfoliation, brightening, and texture improvement. Glycolic acid provides stronger exfoliation while lactic acid suits sensitive or dry skin. AHAs work best for those without significant acne congestion. Those with normal to dry skin benefit from AHA's surface exfoliation combined with hydrating properties.

When to Choose BHA

Choose salicylic acid for acne-prone, oily, or congestion-prone skin. BHA prevents comedone formation and clears existing congestion. Those with blackheads benefit from BHA's pore-penetrating action. BHA is particularly effective for preventing future breakouts through regular use.

For Most Acne-Prone Skin: Both

The most effective approach for acne uses both AHA and BHA on different days or times. Use BHA daily or every other day to address congestion and prevent breakouts. Use AHA 1-2 times weekly for additional exfoliation and skin texture improvement. This combination addresses acne from multiple angles.

Combining AHA and BHA

Don't combine AHA and BHA in the same step initially, as this can over-exfoliate and irritate. Once skin tolerates both separately, you might use both on the same night if your skin is resilient. However, most people find alternating nights more comfortable and equally effective.

Concentration and Frequency Considerations

Higher concentrations exfoliate more strongly but increase irritation risk. Lower concentrations work better for sensitive skin and beginners. Frequency matters more than concentration; consistent 2-3 times weekly use outperforms occasional high-strength treatments. Adjust based on your skin's response.

Beyond Acne: AHA and BHA Benefits

Both promote collagen production and anti-aging effects through increased cell turnover. Both improve skin tone and reduce hyperpigmentation. Both enhance absorption of other skincare products by removing dead skin. These benefits make both valuable additions to comprehensive skincare routines.
