Microdermabrasion

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Microdermabrasion Overview

Microdermabrasion is a gentler alternative to dermabrasion that uses microcrystals or a diamond-tipped wand to mechanically exfoliate the skin's outer layers. It safely addresses fine lines, dull skin, and minor imperfections while promoting cellular renewal and collagen production. It is one of the most accessible and frequently recommended professional facial treatments.

How Microdermabrasion Works

Two microdermabrasion modalities exist. Crystal microdermabrasion uses aluminum oxide or sodium bicarbonate crystals propelled against skin by air pressure to abrade the stratum corneum and epidermis. Diamond-tip microdermabrasion uses a wand with a diamond-textured tip that mechanically abrades skin while simultaneously vacuuming away dead cells. Both methods gently remove the outermost skin layer, promoting skin renewal without deep tissue damage.

Indications for Treatment

Microdermabrasion treats:

  • Fine lines and superficial wrinkles
  • Dull, dehydrated, and tired-looking skin
  • Mild acne scars
  • Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation
  • Rough skin texture
  • Mild hyperpigmentation and age spots
  • General skin revitalization and rejuvenation

The Procedure

Microdermabrasion requires no anesthesia. The face is thoroughly cleansed. The practitioner applies a topical product to enhance gliding. The wand or crystal applicator is moved systematically across the face using light pressure. The process is gentle and well-tolerated, with most patients finding it relaxing. Treatment duration is typically 20-30 minutes. Immediate post-treatment sensation is mild warmth and slight tightness.

Results and Recovery

Results are immediate and cumulative. Skin appears brighter and smoother immediately after treatment. Fine lines appear softer, and overall skin texture improves noticeably. Results continue to improve over several days as cellular turnover increases. There is minimal downtime with only slight redness immediately post-treatment that resolves within hours. Mild peeling may occur over 2-3 days. Patients can apply makeup and resume normal activities immediately.

Treatment Series

While single treatments provide visible improvement, a series of 6-12 treatments spaced 2-4 weeks apart is typically recommended for optimal results. Regular monthly maintenance treatments help sustain results and continued skin improvement. Results plateau over time, with monthly maintenance providing optimal ongoing benefits.

Cost Considerations

Microdermabrasion costs $100-200 per treatment. A 6-treatment series costs $600-1,200. Monthly maintenance treatments cost $100-200. Package pricing often offers discounts for multiple sessions. Insurance does not cover cosmetic microdermabrasion.

Risks and Complications

Microdermabrasion is extremely safe with minimal risks. Minor temporary effects include redness, mild swelling, and increased sensitivity. Rare complications include infection, minor bleeding, and temporary hyperpigmentation. Unlike deeper resurfacing procedures, serious scarring and permanent pigmentation changes are exceptionally rare. Contraindications are minimal. Patients with active acne or infections should postpone treatment. Very sensitive individuals may experience temporary irritation. Overall, microdermabrasion has an excellent safety profile and is appropriate for most skin types.
