Laser Hair Removal

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Laser Hair Removal Overview

Laser hair removal is a highly effective, non-invasive method for reducing unwanted body and facial hair. Using concentrated light energy, lasers selectively destroy hair follicles while sparing surrounding skin. It is the most commonly performed cosmetic procedure in the United States, offering long-lasting results with minimal discomfort and downtime.

How Lasers Remove Hair

Hair removal lasers emit specific wavelengths of light that are preferentially absorbed by melanin in the hair shaft and follicle. The absorbed light energy is converted to heat, damaging the follicle's growth cells (bulge and matrix) and destroying its ability to produce new hair. Multiple laser types with different wavelengths (alexandrite, diode, Nd:YAG, ruby) are available, each suited to different hair colors and skin types. Proper wavelength selection based on patient skin tone and hair color is critical.

Indications for Treatment

Laser hair removal addresses:

  • Unwanted facial hair (upper lip, chin, sideburns)
  • Leg, underarm, and bikini area hair
  • Back and chest hair
  • Hair following trauma or in surgical scars
  • Individuals with dark, coarse hair and light skin
  • Patients seeking permanent or long-lasting hair reduction

The Treatment Series

Multiple treatments are necessary because hair grows in cycles, and lasers only effectively treat hair in the active growth phase. Most patients require 6-10 treatments spaced 4-8 weeks apart for satisfactory results. Treatment intervals depend on the body area (facial hair cycles faster than leg hair). Cumulative hair reduction of 80-90% is typical, though results vary by hair color, skin type, and individual factors.

The Procedure

The treatment area is shaved beforehand. Topical anesthetic or cooling is applied for comfort. The patient wears protective eyewear. The laser handpiece is positioned against the skin, and pulses of light energy are delivered systematically across the treatment area. Patients feel warmth and occasional snapping sensations. Most treatments take 15-45 minutes depending on area size. Discomfort is minimal to moderate.

Results and Recovery

Hair falls out over 2-3 weeks following treatment. Temporary redness and swelling are common immediately post-treatment, resolving within 24 hours. Some temporary darkening of treated skin may occur, resolving over weeks. Patients can resume normal activities immediately. Strict sun protection (SPF 50+) is essential for 2-4 weeks post-treatment and ongoing. Results are long-lasting, though maintenance treatments may be necessary due to new hair growth or hormonal influences.

Cost Considerations

Laser hair removal costs $200-500 per treatment depending on area size and location. A typical 6-treatment series costs $1,200-3,000. Larger areas like legs cost more than smaller areas like upper lip. Geographic location and facility prestige impact pricing. Insurance does not cover cosmetic laser hair removal.

Risks and Complications

Laser hair removal is safe when performed by trained professionals. Common temporary effects include redness, swelling, and temporary hyperpigmentation. Rare complications include blistering, scarring, and paradoxical hair growth. Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation is the most common complication in darker skin types. Proper laser selection, settings adjustment, and skin type assessment are critical. Contraindications include active infections, recent sun exposure, certain medications, and pregnancy. Patients should verify practitioner certification and experience.
