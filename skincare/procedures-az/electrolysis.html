Procedures A-Z Reference

Electrolysis

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Electrolysis Overview

Electrolysis is a permanent hair removal method that uses electrical current to destroy the hair follicle at the root. It is the only FDA-approved method for permanent hair removal and is effective on all hair colors and skin types, making it an excellent option for individuals with gray or light hair unsuitable for laser treatment.

How Electrolysis Works

Three electrolysis modalities exist: galvanic (DC current creating alkaline chemical reaction), thermolytic (radiofrequency creating heat), and blend (combination of galvanic and thermolytic). Each method damages the hair follicle's growth cells, preventing regrowth. The practitioner inserts a fine probe into the hair follicle and applies electrical current for a fraction of a second. The follicle is then epilated (tweezed out), and the process repeats for each hair.

Indications for Treatment

Electrolysis is suitable for:

  • Permanent hair removal on any body area
  • Gray, white, or blonde hair unsuitable for laser
  • Small or limited treatment areas
  • Patients with certain skin types unable to use laser safely
  • Eyebrow shaping and facial hair removal
  • Maintenance after laser hair removal

The Procedure

Electrolysis requires multiple sessions, typically 6-12 treatments or more depending on hair density and follicle development. Sessions last 15-60 minutes. Topical anesthetic is often applied for comfort. The practitioner inserts a probe into each follicle, applies current, and removes the hair. The process is meticulous and time-consuming. Appointments are typically scheduled every 1-4 weeks. Treatment must target hair in the active growth phase, necessitating multiple passes over the same area.

Recovery and Results

Immediately after treatment, the area appears red and may be slightly swollen, resolving within 24 hours. Patients can resume normal activities immediately. Gradual hair reduction occurs with each session. Treated hairs permanently shed, though continued sessions target new hair growth. Complete hair removal typically requires 12-24+ months depending on hair density and body area. Results are permanent for treated follicles, though new hair growth or hormonal changes may require maintenance treatments.

Cost Considerations

Electrolysis costs $50-150 per treatment session depending on area size and session duration. Complete treatment for extensive areas can total $1,500-3,000+ over the course of therapy. Many practitioners offer package pricing for multiple sessions. Insurance typically does not cover cosmetic electrolysis.

Risks and Complications

Electrolysis is safe when performed by trained professionals. Common temporary effects include redness, swelling, and scabbing. Potential complications include infection, scarring, hyperpigmentation, hypopigmentation, and regrowth of treated hairs. Folliculitis can occur if proper aftercare is not followed. Severe burns or permanent scarring are rare with properly trained practitioners. Patients should ensure treatment providers are licensed electrologists certified through recognized organizations.
