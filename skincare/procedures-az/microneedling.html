Microneedling

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Microneedling Overview

Microneedling, also called collagen induction therapy, uses multiple fine needles to create controlled micro-injuries that stimulate the body's natural collagen and elastin production. This minimally invasive procedure effectively improves skin texture, reduces scars and wrinkles, and addresses various dermatological concerns with minimal downtime.

How Microneedling Works

Microneedling devices use fine needles to create precise punctures in the skin at controlled depths (typically 0.5-2.5mm). These micro-injuries trigger the body's wound healing cascade, initiating inflammatory, proliferative, and remodeling phases. This process stimulates robust collagen and elastin production, improving skin structure from within. The epidermis remains largely intact, preserving skin barrier function while promoting dermal remodeling.

Indications for Treatment

Microneedling effectively treats:

  • Atrophic scars (ice pick, boxcar, and rolling acne scars)
  • Traumatic scars
  • Fine lines and wrinkles
  • Loss of skin elasticity and laxity
  • Uneven skin texture and roughness
  • Stretch marks
  • Alopecia (hair loss)
  • Hyperpigmentation and discoloration

The Procedure

Topical numbing cream is applied for 30-45 minutes before treatment. The microneedling device is gently rolled or stamped across the treatment area in multiple directions. Practitioners adjust needle depth based on target concern and skin type. The procedure causes minimal discomfort due to anesthesia, with most patients describing a prickling sensation. Treatment typically takes 30-45 minutes. A single pass may be made for milder cases, or multiple passes for more aggressive treatment.

Results and Timeline

Mild immediate improvement is visible from the inflammatory response and tissue swelling. True results develop progressively over 4-12 weeks as collagen remodeling occurs. Maximum results from a single treatment appear at 8-12 weeks. Progressive improvement continues for up to 6 months. Optimal results typically require a series of 3-6 treatments spaced 4-6 weeks apart, depending on condition severity and desired results.

Recovery and Aftercare

Redness and mild swelling peak at 24-48 hours post-treatment and typically resolve within 3-5 days. Some practitioners achieve more dramatic results with aggressive techniques, resulting in longer recovery. Makeup can typically be applied after 24 hours. Patients should avoid sun exposure and use strict sun protection (SPF 50+) for at least 2 weeks. Strenuous exercise should be avoided for 48 hours. The skin barrier typically normalizes within 5-7 days. Continued hydration and gentle skincare support healing.

Cost Considerations

Microneedling costs range from $200-500 per treatment depending on needle length, device technology, and treatment area. A series of 3-6 treatments costs $600-3,000. Some facilities offer package pricing. Insurance typically does not cover cosmetic microneedling, though therapeutic treatment of scars may have partial coverage.

Risks and Complications

Microneedling is generally safe. Common temporary effects include redness, swelling, and tenderness. Minor complications include temporary dryness, post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, and infection if proper aftercare is not followed. Serious complications including scarring and permanent pigmentation changes are rare with appropriate needle depths and technique. Contraindications include active infections, severe cystic acne, and certain medications. Patients prone to keloids should use caution or avoid treatment. Practitioners should maintain strict sterile technique to prevent infection.
