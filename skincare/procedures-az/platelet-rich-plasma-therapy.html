Platelet-Rich Plasma Therapy

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Platelet-Rich Plasma Therapy Overview

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy harnesses the healing power of growth factors concentrated in blood platelets to stimulate tissue repair and regeneration. Derived from the patient's own blood, PRP is used to address skin aging, scars, hair loss, and various dermatological conditions with minimal risk of allergic reaction.

How PRP Works

Whole blood is drawn and centrifuged to separate platelets and growth factors from other blood components. The concentrated PRP is rich in growth factors including platelet-derived growth factor (PDGF), vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF), and transforming growth factor-beta (TGF-β). When injected or applied to skin, these growth factors stimulate fibroblast activity, collagen production, angiogenesis (new blood vessel formation), and cellular regeneration. The results are gradual tissue improvement and rejuvenation.

Indications for PRP Therapy

PRP effectively treats:

  • Facial aging and loss of volume
  • Fine lines and wrinkles
  • Acne and traumatic scars
  • Skin texture irregularities
  • Androgenetic alopecia (hair loss)
  • Post-surgical scar revision
  • Sun-damaged and photoaged skin
  • Dark under-eye circles

The Procedure

The procedure begins with blood draw (typically 9-15 mL). Blood is processed in a centrifuge to concentrate platelets. The centrifugation process takes 10-15 minutes. PRP is then injected directly into targeted areas using a fine needle or applied topically to areas treated with microneedling. For facial rejuvenation, multiple small injections are placed throughout the face. For hair loss, multiple injections are administered to the scalp in affected areas. The process is relatively painless with minimal anesthesia required.

Results and Timeline

Results develop gradually over 4-12 weeks as growth factors stimulate tissue regeneration. Initial results may be subtle but continue improving progressively. Maximum results typically appear at 12 weeks. Continued improvement can occur for up to 6 months. Results are less dramatic than ablative procedures but more natural-appearing. Most patients require 3-4 treatments spaced 4-6 weeks apart for optimal results. Maintenance treatments every 6-12 months help sustain improvements.

Recovery and Downtime

Recovery is minimal with most patients experiencing only mild bruising and swelling at injection sites, resolving within 24-48 hours. Pain is minimal. Patients can resume normal activities immediately. Makeup can be applied after 24 hours. Some redness may be visible briefly. For scalp injections, patients can wash hair normally after 24 hours. Overall downtime is minimal, making PRP suitable for busy individuals.

Cost Considerations

PRP therapy costs $500-2,000 per treatment depending on volume required and treatment area. A series of 3 treatments costs $1,500-6,000. Maintenance treatments typically cost $500-1,500 per session. Geographic location and facility prestige impact pricing. Insurance does not cover cosmetic PRP therapy.

Risks and Complications

PRP therapy is very safe since material is autologous (from the patient's own blood), eliminating rejection and allergic reaction risks. Minimal risks include bruising, swelling, and infection at injection sites. Rare complications include nerve injury from injection into sensitive areas. No systemic complications have been reported. The main drawback is that results are less dramatic than more aggressive procedures. Efficacy varies significantly between individuals and treatment protocols. Currently, research continues on optimal PRP preparation and application methods to improve consistency of results.
