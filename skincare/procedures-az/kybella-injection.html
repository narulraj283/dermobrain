Kybella Injection

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Kybella Overview

Kybella is an FDA-approved injectable treatment for submental fullness (double chin). It contains deoxycholic acid, a naturally occurring substance that breaks down dietary fat. Injected directly into the fat beneath the chin, Kybella permanently destroys fat cells, providing non-surgical chin contouring without liposuction.

How Kybella Works

Deoxycholic acid disrupts the cell membrane of fat cells, causing them to rupture and leak their contents. The body's immune system then removes these damaged fat cells through normal metabolic processes. Once destroyed, fat cells cannot regenerate, providing permanent fat reduction. The process takes several weeks as the body gradually eliminates damaged cells.

Indications for Treatment

Kybella is appropriate for:

  • Submental fullness (double chin) unresponsive to diet and exercise
  • Patients seeking non-surgical alternative to liposuction
  • Those with BMI under 30
  • Patients with genetics causing predisposition to submental fat
  • Those seeking improvement in profile definition

The Procedure

Multiple small injections are administered directly into the submental fat using a fine needle. The chin area is first marked with a grid pattern to ensure accurate, evenly-spaced injections. The injection process takes 15-20 minutes. Topical anesthetic or ice is applied beforehand. Most patients tolerate injections well, experiencing minimal discomfort. A single treatment typically involves 20-50 injections depending on chin size and desired results. Sessions are spaced 4 weeks apart.

Results and Timeline

Results develop gradually over 4-8 weeks as the body eliminates fat cells. Most patients see noticeable improvement after 2-3 treatments. Maximum results typically appear at 3-6 months. Results are cumulative, with multiple treatments providing progressive improvement. Once fat cells are destroyed, results are permanent, though weight gain can enlarge remaining fat cells.

Recovery and Aftercare

Recovery is straightforward with minimal downtime. Swelling, bruising, and tenderness are common in the first 24-48 hours. Some patients experience temporary difficulty swallowing due to swelling. Ice application and head elevation help reduce swelling. Pain is minimal and managed with over-the-counter analgesia. Most patients can return to work and normal activities within 24-48 hours. Strenuous exercise should be avoided for 5-7 days.

Cost Considerations

Kybella costs $600-800 per treatment session. Most patients require 2-6 treatments over several months, with total treatment costs ranging from $1,200-4,800. Some facilities offer package pricing for multiple sessions. Insurance does not cover cosmetic chin contouring with Kybella.

Risks and Complications

Kybella is FDA-approved with an excellent safety profile. Common temporary effects include swelling, bruising, redness, numbness, and mild discomfort. Rare serious complications include nerve damage causing prolonged numbness or jaw pain, and infection. Temporary difficulty swallowing has been reported due to swelling. Swelling is usually manageable with ice and elevation. Most patients experience only minor temporary side effects. Not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
