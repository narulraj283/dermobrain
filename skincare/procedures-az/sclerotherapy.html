Sclerotherapy

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Sclerotherapy Overview

Sclerotherapy is a minimally invasive treatment for varicose veins and spider veins that involves injecting a sclerosing agent directly into affected veins. The solution causes vein wall inflammation and closure, with the vessel gradually reabsorbing and fading. It provides excellent cosmetic results and symptom relief with minimal downtime.

How Sclerotherapy Works

Sclerosing agents (most commonly sodium tetradecyl sulfate or polidocanol) are injected into targeted veins. The agent damages the vein endothelium, triggering inflammation and thrombosis. The inflamed vessel walls stick together, sealing the vein. Blood flow redirects to healthy veins. Over weeks to months, the closed vein is reabsorbed by the body and fades from view. The result is elimination of visible veins and often symptom improvement.

Indications for Sclerotherapy

Sclerotherapy treats:

  • Spider veins (small telangiectatic veins)
  • Varicose veins (larger dilated veins)
  • Reticular veins (small veins feeding spider veins)
  • Vein-related discomfort and heaviness
  • Post-thrombotic syndrome veins
  • Patients seeking improved appearance and symptom relief

The Procedure

Sclerotherapy is performed in an office setting without anesthesia. The treatment area is marked and cleaned. The practitioner visualizes veins using transillumination or ultrasound (for deeper veins). A fine needle is carefully positioned in the target vein, and sclerosing agent is slowly injected. Patients feel minimal discomfort, often describing only slight burning. Compression is applied immediately. Multiple veins can be injected during a single session. Total procedure time typically takes 15-30 minutes depending on the extent of treatment.

Results and Recovery

Immediate results are not visible. Veins typically fade progressively over 2-6 weeks as the body reabsorbs them. Some veins may require 2-3 injections for complete closure. Most patients see significant improvement within 4 weeks. Complete results from a full treatment series appear at 3-6 months. Matting (fine red lines at injection sites) occasionally occurs but usually fades over months. Results are typically permanent for treated veins, though new spider veins may develop over time.

Compression and Aftercare

Compression stockings are worn for 24-72 hours after treatment to maintain vein closure and reduce bruising and swelling. Mild bruising, redness, and temporary itching at injection sites are normal. Patients should avoid strenuous exercise and heat exposure for 48 hours. Walking is encouraged. Elevation of treated areas helps reduce swelling. Most patients resume normal activities within 24 hours. Mild discomfort managed with over-the-counter analgesia is common.

Cost Considerations

Sclerotherapy costs range from $200-400 per treatment session, with most patients requiring 2-6 sessions. Complete treatment typically costs $400-2,400. Session costs depend on the number and extent of veins treated. Insurance may cover sclerotherapy if varicose veins cause symptoms or medical problems, though spider vein treatment is usually cosmetic and not covered.

Risks and Complications

Sclerotherapy is generally safe. Common temporary effects include bruising, redness, itching, and swelling at injection sites. Rare serious complications include deep vein thrombosis, arterial injection causing tissue damage, and allergic reactions to sclerosing agent. Hyperpigmentation or temporary post-inflammatory changes can occur. Matting (fine red lines from new telangiectasia) occasionally develops but usually fades. Recurrent veins can occur in treated areas. Adequate compression post-treatment helps prevent complications. Practitioners should have proper training in sclerotherapy techniques and emergency protocols.
