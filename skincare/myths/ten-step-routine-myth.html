Dermatology Myths vs Facts

Skincare Myth: You Need a 10-Step Routine

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

The Myth

Social media and skincare marketing have popularized the idea that an extensive skincare routine with 10+ steps is necessary for healthy, beautiful skin. The "10-step Korean skincare routine" became particularly trendy, leading many people to believe that more products equal better skin.

The Reality

Dermatologists agree: you don't need a 10-step routine for healthy skin. In fact, excessive products can be counterproductive:

  • More products = more potential for irritation and adverse reactions
  • Complicated routines reduce compliance (people don't stick with them)
  • Many steps are redundant
  • Over-treating skin can damage the barrier and cause problems
  • Simpler routines are more effective for most people

The Minimum Effective Routine

Most people can achieve excellent skin with just 3-5 steps:

  • Cleanser: Morning and evening to remove dirt and oil
  • Treatment Product (optional): Salicylic acid, benzoyl peroxide, retinoid, or vitamin C—choose one based on your main concern
  • Moisturizer: Day and night for hydration
  • Sunscreen: Every morning (essential, not optional)
  • Targeted Treatment (optional): Second active ingredient if addressing multiple concerns

That's 4-5 products, not 10.

The Problem with 10-Step Routines

Overwhelming: Most people can't maintain complex routines consistently. A simple routine you'll actually use beats an elaborate one you abandon.

Redundant: Many steps perform similar functions (multiple cleansing waters, multiple essences, multiple serums).

Irritation Risk: Using 10 products dramatically increases the risk of irritation, breakouts, and barrier damage.

Cost: 10 products gets expensive quickly, which isn't necessary for skin health.

Time-Consuming: A 10-step routine takes 20-30 minutes daily. Most people lack this time.

Where the 10-Step Trend Came From

The 10-step Korean skincare routine includes:

  1. Oil cleanser
  2. Water cleanser
  3. Exfoliator
  4. Toner
  5. Essence
  6. Emulsion
  7. Sheet mask
  8. Serum
  9. Eye cream
  10. Moisturizer + sunscreen

While some steps serve purposes, most people don't need all of them. The routine was popularized globally through marketing, not dermatological necessity.

What Actually Matters

  • Consistency: Using the same simple routine daily beats sporadically using many products
  • Right Products for Your Skin: One effective product beats 10 mediocre ones
  • Active Ingredients: One proven active (retinoid, vitamin C, salicylic acid) beats 10 products with no actives
  • Sunscreen: This is non-negotiable and essential
  • Moisturizer: Everyone needs this regardless of skin type

Building Your Ideal Routine

Start minimally and add only what you need:

  • Step 1—Cleanser: Choose one suitable for your skin type
  • Step 2—Sunscreen (AM): Non-negotiable
  • Step 3—Moisturizer: Essential for all skin types
  • Step 4—Active Treatment: Add if targeting specific concerns (acne, aging, etc.)
  • Step 5—Optional Booster: Add second treatment only if needed and well-tolerated

That's 4-5 steps. Stop there unless dermatologist recommends otherwise.

The Pareto Principle Applies to Skincare

The 80/20 rule: 80% of skincare results come from 20% of effort. Typically:

  • Sunscreen = 40% of anti-aging results
  • Cleanser + moisturizer = 30% of results
  • Active ingredient = 20% of results
  • Additional products = 10% of results (diminishing returns)

Bottom Line

You don't need 10 steps for beautiful, healthy skin. Most people achieve excellent results with a simple 3-5 step routine: cleanser, treatment (if needed), moisturizer, sunscreen, and minimal additional products. Simpler routines are more sustainable, less irritating, and often more effective than complex ones.
