What Is Total Nail Avulsion: Complete Guide to the Procedure?

If you're considering this treatment, you probably have questions about what it is, how it works, and what results you can expect. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know in plain English—no medical jargon required. Thousands of people choose to have this procedure performed each year.

How Does It Work?

This procedure works by treating your skin at a deeper level to create lasting improvements. Your dermatologist will use specialized techniques to target the specific concerns you want to address. Most people describe the experience as manageable, and many see results improve over the following weeks and months.

Your skin has a natural ability to heal and regenerate. This treatment takes advantage of that by stimulating your body's own repair mechanisms. When your skin heals, it produces new collagen and creates fresh, healthy skin cells. That's why results often improve over time rather than immediately after your appointment.

What To Expect Before, During, and After

Before Your Appointment: Your dermatologist will review your medical history and discuss your goals. They'll ask about medications you're taking and whether you have any skin sensitivities. Most people don't need special preparation, though you'll want to avoid sun exposure for a few days beforehand.

During the Procedure: The appointment typically takes 15-60 minutes depending on the treatment area size. You'll be awake and comfortable throughout. Most people experience minimal discomfort, though you might feel pressure, warmth, or slight tingling. Your dermatologist can discuss pain management options if you're concerned.

After the Procedure: You can usually return to normal activities immediately. Your skin might be red or slightly swollen for hours to days. This is completely normal and part of healing. Your dermatologist will give you specific aftercare instructions to help you get the best results.

When Will You See Results?

Most people notice initial improvements within the first few days to a week. However, the best results typically develop over 2-4 weeks as your skin continues to heal and regenerate. Some treatments show improvement for up to three months as your body naturally produces more collagen.

Depending on your specific treatment, you might need multiple sessions spaced 4-6 weeks apart for optimal results. Your dermatologist will recommend the best schedule during your consultation. Using sunscreen daily and following your dermatologist's skincare recommendations will help maintain your results.

Is This Treatment Right For You?

This procedure works well for most people. Your dermatologist will discuss whether you're a good candidate during your consultation. Generally, you should be in good overall health and have realistic expectations about what the treatment can achieve.

Certain medical conditions, medications, or skin types might mean you need to postpone treatment or choose an alternative option. Your dermatologist might recommend waiting if you have active skin infections or take certain medications. That's why a thorough consultation is important.

Safety and Side Effects

When performed by a trained, board-certified dermatologist, this procedure is very safe. Like any treatment, there can be mild temporary side effects. You might experience redness, swelling, slight tenderness, or mild itching. These typically resolve within hours to a few days.

Serious side effects are uncommon when performed correctly. Your dermatologist will discuss all possible risks and benefits during your consultation. Follow all aftercare instructions to minimize complications and get the best possible results.

Cost and Insurance

Most cosmetic procedures are not covered by insurance since they're considered elective treatments. However, some treatments may be covered if they're medically necessary. During your consultation, ask your dermatologist's office about costs and financing options that might be available.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many treatments will I need?

Most people see good results after 1-3 treatments. This varies depending on your specific needs and desired results. Your dermatologist will recommend a personalized plan during your consultation.

How long do results last?

Results vary depending on the treatment and your individual factors. Many treatments last 6-12 months. Some people choose maintenance sessions to keep results looking their best.

Will it hurt?

Most people report minimal discomfort. You might feel pressure, warmth, or mild tingling, but true pain is uncommon. Your dermatologist can discuss pain management options if needed.

Can I wear makeup after?

Usually, yes, but timing depends on the specific treatment. Most dermatologists recommend waiting a few hours. Use clean applicators and gentle products while your skin is healing.
