Surgical Procedures & Techniques

Pilar Cyst Removal: Surgical Approach

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Pilar Cysts: Characteristics and Clinical Features

Pilar cysts, also known as trichilemmal cysts, are common benign lesions that arise from the outer root sheath of hair follicles. They typically occur on the scalp and appear as firm, mobile nodules. Unlike epidermoid cysts, pilar cysts have a thicker, more fibrous wall and contain a homogeneous keratinous material without a defined punctum.

Indications for Removal

Removal is indicated for cosmetic concerns, large size that may cause discomfort or hair loss, recurrent inflammation, or patient preference. Pilar cysts do not malignantly transform, so removal is purely elective in most cases.

Surgical Excision Technique

Scalp Location Considerations

The scalp's rich vascular supply and hair-bearing nature require modified surgical approach. A linear incision is made over the cyst, typically in a superior-inferior orientation to allow favorable scar orientation with hair growth pattern. Local anesthesia with epinephrine is essential for hemostasis.

Cyst Removal Steps

Once anesthetized, the incision is made through skin and underlying tissue. Careful dissection along the cyst capsule allows for complete removal while minimizing damage to surrounding healthy tissue. The dense fibrous wall of pilar cysts requires careful handling to avoid rupture.

After complete extraction, the wound bed is irrigated. Hair follicles and sebaceous glands that are not damaged should be preserved. Closure is performed with interrupted absorbable sutures in the deeper layers and non-absorbable sutures in the skin, taking care to avoid hair follicles during suture placement.

Multiple Cyst Management

Patients with multiple pilar cysts can have all lesions removed in a single procedure if they are in proximity. This reduces overall operative time and wound healing period. However, large numbers of cysts may warrant staged procedures to minimize operative time and wound burden.

Post-operative Care on Scalp

Wound care is critical on the scalp due to the environment and hair. Patients should keep the area clean and dry, avoiding shampooing the immediate wound area until suture removal. Sutures are removed at 10-14 days depending on healing. The risk of infection on the scalp is higher than other sites, requiring vigilant monitoring.

Recurrence Rates

Complete cyst wall removal minimizes recurrence, with rates typically less than 5%. Recurrent cysts should be managed with repeat excision, ensuring complete capsule removal.
