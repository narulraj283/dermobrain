Mohs & Skin Cancer Surgery

Mohs Surgery Recovery Timeline and Aftercare

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Understanding the Recovery Process

Recovery from Mohs micrographic surgery is typically straightforward, with most patients returning to normal activities within a few days. However, complete wound healing requires several weeks, and optimal scar appearance develops over several months.

Immediate Post-Operative Period (Days 1-3)

In the first three days after surgery, focus on keeping the wound clean and dry. The surgical site may have mild bleeding or oozing, which is normal. Change dressings as directed by your surgeon, typically daily. Pain is usually minimal and controlled with over-the-counter analgesics. Avoid getting the wound wet or submerged in water for the first 48 hours.

First Two Weeks After Surgery

During the first two weeks, continue daily wound care with gentle cleansing and dressing changes. Sutures are typically removed 7-10 days after surgery, depending on the location. Wounds on the face heal fastest, while those on the trunk or extremities may require longer before suture removal. After suture removal, continue wound care until the wound is fully closed.

Weeks 3-6 Post-Operative

By three weeks, most wounds are closed and healing continues beneath the skin surface. Scar appearance begins to improve as the skin remodels. Avoid strenuous exercise, heavy lifting, and activities that cause excessive sweating or that could traumatize the wound. Sun protection is critical during this period.

Months 2-12: Scar Maturation

The most dramatic improvement in scar appearance occurs during the first 3-6 months as the scar matures and becomes less red or dark. The scar continues to improve gradually for up to one year. Silicone-based products or pressure garments may help optimize scar appearance during this phase.

Return to Activities Timeline

  • Day after surgery: Light activities, desk work
  • 1 week: Gentle exercise, avoiding heavy sweating
  • 2-3 weeks: Return to moderate exercise
  • 4-6 weeks: Return to strenuous exercise and sports

When to Contact Your Surgeon

Contact your surgeon if you experience signs of infection (increasing pain, warmth, redness, discharge), excessive bleeding, signs of allergic reaction, or severe swelling that does not improve with elevation and ice.
