Mohs & Skin Cancer Surgery

Choosing a Mohs Surgeon: What to Look For

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

The Importance of Surgeon Selection

Mohs micrographic surgery is a specialized procedure requiring extensive training and experience. The quality of the surgeon significantly impacts outcomes. Selecting an appropriately trained and experienced Mohs surgeon is an important decision for patients with skin cancer.

Board Certification and Training

The surgeon should be certified by the American Board of Dermatology (ABD) in dermatology. Additional specialized training in dermatologic surgery, preferably including Mohs fellowship training, is important. Many Mohs surgeons complete an additional 1-2 year Mohs fellowship after dermatology residency.

Credentials to Verify

  • American Board of Dermatology (ABD) certification in dermatology
  • Fellowship training in dermatologic surgery or Mohs micrographic surgery
  • Membership in the American College of Mohs Surgery (ACMS)
  • State medical license in good standing
  • Malpractice insurance history

Experience and Volume

Surgeon experience is critical. A surgeon should perform a significant volume of Mohs procedures to maintain expertise. Surgeons performing fewer than 500 Mohs cases annually may have lower experience levels. Ask your surgeon about their annual case volume.

Facility and Equipment

The surgical facility should be properly equipped with a dedicated Mohs laboratory. Equipment should include a cryostat, microscopes, and staining capabilities. The facility should meet standards for outpatient surgical facilities and maintain appropriate infection control practices.

Cosmetic and Reconstructive Expertise

Mohs surgery removes the cancer, but the surgeon must also reconstruct the resulting defect. Look for a surgeon with expertise in reconstruction techniques including flaps, grafts, and complex closures. This expertise is particularly important for defects on the face.

Patient Outcomes and Satisfaction

Ask the surgeon about their recurrence rates and complication rates. While some variation is expected, recurrence rates significantly higher than published benchmarks warrant further investigation. Patient reviews and referrals from other healthcare providers can provide insight into satisfaction levels.

Communication and Patient Education

The surgeon should communicate clearly about the procedure, risks, benefits, and alternatives. Time spent in consultation discussing your specific lesion and concerns is important. A surgeon who takes time to educate patients about their condition is preferable.

Accessibility and Logistics

Consider practical factors such as office location, availability for follow-up care, and accessibility for patients with mobility limitations. Mohs surgery may require multiple visits if additional treatment layers are needed.

Insurance and Cost Considerations

Verify that the surgeon's office accepts your insurance. Understand the cost of the procedure and any out-of-pocket expenses. While cost should not be the primary factor, it is reasonable to understand financial implications.

Seeking Referrals

Ask your primary care physician or dermatologist for referrals to reputable Mohs surgeons. Professional referrals often indicate surgeon reputation and quality. Online resources such as the American College of Mohs Surgery website provide surgeon locator tools.
