Mohs & Skin Cancer Surgery

What to Expect on Your Mohs Surgery Day

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Preparing for Mohs Surgery

Mohs micrographic surgery is an outpatient procedure performed in an office-based surgical suite. Understanding what to expect on surgery day helps reduce anxiety and ensures you are well-prepared for the experience.

Timeline for the Day

Plan to spend 2-4 hours at the surgical facility, though procedures can occasionally take longer depending on the complexity of the lesion. Bring reading materials or entertainment, as there will be waiting periods while tissue is being processed and examined.

Arrival and Check-in

Arrive 15 minutes early to check in. Inform staff about any medications you are taking, allergies, or medical conditions. If you are taking blood thinners, inform the surgeon before the procedure begins. Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that allows easy access to the surgical site.

Consent and Photographic Documentation

Before surgery begins, you will sign consent forms and the surgeon will answer final questions. Photographs of the lesion will be taken for surgical documentation and comparison with post-operative results.

Local Anesthesia Administration

Local anesthesia is injected around the lesion. You may feel a burning sensation for a few seconds as the anesthetic is administered, but this quickly subsides. Within 2-3 minutes, the area is completely numb and you should feel no pain during the procedure, only pressure sensations and vibration from instruments.

The Waiting Period

After the initial tissue removal, you will wait while the Mohs laboratory processes and examines the specimen. This typically takes 45 minutes to an hour. This waiting period is the longest part of the procedure and allows the surgical team to determine if additional tissue removal is needed.

Reconstruction Phase

Once the surgeon confirms clear margins, reconstruction begins. You may hear the sound of cautery instruments or feel sutures being placed, but you should not feel pain due to the local anesthesia.

Discharge Instructions

After completion, you will receive detailed wound care instructions. Most patients can return to normal activities the following day, though strenuous exercise should be avoided for one week.
