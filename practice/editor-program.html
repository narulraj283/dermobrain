    Frequently Asked Questions

    How much time does reviewing an article take? ▼
    Most articles take 5-15 minutes to review, depending on length and your familiarity with the topic. You choose how many you want to review—there's no pressure or quota.
    Will my name appear on articles? ▼
    Yes. Your name, credentials, photo, and a link to your practice website will be displayed prominently on every article you review, marked as "Medically Reviewed by Dr. [Your Name]."
    What's the SEO benefit of the backlink? ▼
    DermoBrain receives strong organic traffic from search engines and has high domain authority. The backlink to your practice from these articles can boost your practice website's SEO and visibility. We use proper HTML attribution with rel="author" tags.
    How do I get the badge for my website? ▼
    Once you review your first article, you'll receive an embed code for your badge. You can copy-paste it into your website's HTML, and the badge will display with your name, tier, and number of articles reviewed. It auto-updates as you review more articles.
    Can I pick just specific topics to review? ▼
    Absolutely. You select your specialty areas during application, and you'll only see articles in those categories. You can also manually filter by topic when browsing articles to review.
    What if I find an error in an article? ▼
    You can suggest edits directly in the review interface. Our editorial team will review your feedback and implement corrections as needed. You'll still be credited as a reviewer even if changes are made.
    Is there any cost to participate? ▼
    No, it's completely free. You get the badges, backlink, and all the benefits at no cost. It's our way of recognizing and supporting dermatologists who contribute to better patient education.
    How do I advance to higher tiers? ▼
    Tiers advance automatically based on the number of articles you review. Review 5+ articles to become a Senior Reviewer, 15+ for Editorial Board, and we invite top contributors to Editor-in-Chief. There's no application process—just keep reviewing!
    Can I see my reviewer profile? ▼
    Yes. Once you join, you'll have a public profile on DermoBrain showing your bio, credentials, articles reviewed, and tier status. Senior Reviewers and above are also featured on our Contributors page.
    What are the verification requirements? ▼
    We verify that you hold a valid, current board certification in dermatology from a recognized board (ABMS, AOA, etc.). Your credentials are checked before you're activated in the program. This ensures credibility for both reviewers and DermoBrain.
