Aveli Cellulite Treatment: Innovative Subcision and Radiofrequency System

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Understanding Aveli Technology

Aveli is a newer minimally invasive cellulite treatment system that combines mechanical subcision with continuous radiofrequency energy delivery. FDA-approved in 2022, Aveli builds on subcision principles while providing enhanced radiofrequency application. The system allows for more efficient treatment with improved consistency and reproducibility compared to earlier subcision devices.

Procedure Mechanism

The Aveli system works through a streamlined approach that:

  • Uses a specialized probe for controlled septal division
  • Delivers continuous radiofrequency energy during subcision
  • Treats multiple dimples efficiently in single treatment sessions
  • Minimizes tissue trauma while maximizing septal disruption

The continuous RF energy application during subcision provides thermal coagulation of cut septae, reducing reattachment potential and enhancing long-term stability.

Treatment Parameters

Aveli treatment typically addresses larger cellulite regions compared to injectable approaches. Standard treatment involves:

  • Treatment of full thigh or buttock regions in single sessions
  • Local anesthesia administration with tumescent fluid injection
  • Multiple probe insertions targeting specific dimple patterns
  • Total treatment time of 45-60 minutes per area

Clinical Efficacy

Clinical trials of Aveli demonstrate significant cellulite improvement with sustained results. Treatment produces immediate dimple elevation and progressive improvement over 8-12 weeks as swelling resolution continues. Long-term follow-up data (12-24 months) shows maintained improvements in the majority of treated patients.

Advantages Over Other Subcision Systems

Aveli's streamlined design and continuous RF integration provide several advantages:

  • More efficient treatment of large cellulite areas
  • Reduced treatment time compared to earlier subcision devices
  • Improved radiofrequency energy delivery consistency
  • Enhanced safety profile with refined probe design

Recovery Timeline

Recovery from Aveli treatment is similar to other minimally invasive subcision approaches:

  • Mild swelling and bruising for 1-2 weeks
  • Small incision sites heal without visible scarring
  • Return to normal activities within 2-3 days
  • Strenuous exercise resumable at 1-2 weeks

Pain is minimal and managed with over-the-counter analgesics. Most patients describe the recovery as easier than anticipated.

Candidacy and Patient Selection

Aveli is ideal for patients with moderate cellulite affecting larger areas (thighs, buttocks) who desire durable results and accept minor downtime. It is particularly suitable for those who have not achieved adequate results with non-invasive treatments or prefer longer-lasting results despite slightly greater invasiveness.

Combination Treatment Strategies

Some practitioners combine Aveli subcision with radiofrequency skin tightening treatments to address both septal disruption and skin quality concerns, potentially providing enhanced aesthetic outcomes.
