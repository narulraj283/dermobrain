QWO Cellulite Treatment: New Collagenase Injection Therapy

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Understanding QWO Technology

QWO (collagenase clostridium histolyticum) is the first and only FDA-approved injectable treatment specifically designed to address cellulite. Approved in 2020, QWO uses collagenase enzyme derived from Clostridium histolyticum bacteria to break down the fibrous septa (connective tissue bands) that create the characteristic dimpled appearance of cellulite.

Mechanism of Action

Cellulite develops when fibrous collagen bands beneath the skin pull downward on the epidermis while protruding fat creates upward pressure, resulting in dimples and irregularities. QWO's collagenase enzyme targets and breaks down these structural collagen septae, reducing the mechanical basis for cellulite appearance. The treatment addresses the underlying anatomy of cellulite rather than merely reducing fat or tightening skin.

Cellulite Classification

QWO is most effective for mild to moderate cellulite (grades 1-2 on the Cellulite Severity Scale). Treatment involves:

  • Injecting QWO directly into dimpled areas
  • Precise depot placement targeting septal structure
  • Multiple injections across affected regions
  • Treatment of both legs or targeted single areas

Treatment Protocol

The standard QWO treatment involves three sessions spaced 21 days apart. Each session targets specific cellulite dimples with injections distributed across the affected area. Treatments are performed on an outpatient basis with minimal preparation required. Practitioners use anatomical landmarks to guide precise septal targeting.

Results and Timeline

Clinical trials demonstrate significant cellulite improvement in the majority of treated patients. Results develop progressively over 3-6 weeks following each injection session. Most patients require the complete three-session series for optimal results. Improvements continue to develop even after treatment completion, with maximum results visible at 3-4 months post-final treatment.

Efficacy Data

In clinical studies, approximately 70% of patients treated with QWO achieved at least one-grade cellulite severity improvement. Approximately 40-50% of patients achieved two-grade improvement. Results appear most pronounced in mild to moderate cellulite cases; severe cellulite may require combination treatment approaches.

Side Effects and Recovery

QWO injections produce localized inflammatory response with expected side effects including:

  • Bruising and swelling at injection sites
  • Localized pain and tenderness
  • Temporary redness and induration
  • Possible temporary firmness or swelling of treated areas

Most side effects resolve within 2-3 weeks. Patients can resume normal activities immediately, though strenuous leg exercise should be minimized for 2-3 days post-treatment. Ice and compression help manage swelling.

Combination Therapy Approach

Many practitioners combine QWO with complementary treatments like radiofrequency, ultrasound, or laser therapies to address multiple aspects of cellulite (structural, skin texture, circulation). This combination approach may produce superior results compared to single modality treatment.
