Cellfina Cellulite Treatment: Subcision for Long-Term Results

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Understanding Subcision Technology

Cellfina is a minimally invasive cellulite treatment that uses radiofrequency-guided subcision to release and break the fibrous septal bands responsible for cellulite dimpling. The procedure combines mechanical separation of septa with bipolar radiofrequency energy to enhance and stabilize results. Approved by the FDA in 2015, Cellfina represents a significant advancement in cellulite treatment technology.

Mechanism of Action

The Cellfina device uses a specialized probe that:

  • Visualizes septa structure under the skin
  • Mechanically disrupts and separates connective tissue bands
  • Applies radiofrequency energy to denature collagen and prevent reattachment
  • Creates space that persists long-term, reducing dimple appearance

By addressing the mechanical basis of cellulite while preventing collagen remodeling and reattachment, Cellfina provides more durable results compared to non-invasive treatments.

Treatment Indications

Cellfina works best for mild to moderate cellulite with clearly defined dimples. The procedure is particularly effective for:

  • Moderate cellulite affecting buttocks and thighs
  • Well-defined dimple patterns
  • Patients desiring longer-lasting results
  • Cases where previous non-invasive treatments provided insufficient improvement

Procedure Details

Cellfina treatment is performed under local or tumescent anesthesia and typically takes 45-60 minutes per treatment area. The procedure involves:

  • Small incisions (3-5 mm) placed strategically in inconspicuous locations
  • Insertion of the specialized probe to target specific dimples
  • Visualization of septal structure and targeted subcision
  • Radiofrequency energy application to prevent reattachment
  • Closure with minimal or no sutures required

Results and Longevity

Cellfina produces immediate visible improvement in treated dimples, with results continuing to improve over 8-12 weeks as swelling resolves. Most importantly, results are long-lasting, with clinical data showing 85% of patients maintained significant improvement at 3 years post-treatment. This exceptional longevity distinguishes Cellfina from non-invasive alternatives.

Recovery and Downtime

Recovery is minimal despite the minimally invasive nature of treatment. Expected post-treatment effects include:

  • Mild swelling and bruising lasting 1-2 weeks
  • Temporary numbness or sensitivity in treated areas
  • Minimal pain managed with over-the-counter analgesics
  • Small incisions heal rapidly without visible scarring

Most patients resume normal activities within 2-3 days, with exercise resumable at 1-2 weeks post-treatment.

Comparison with Other Cellulite Treatments

Cellfina's significant advantage over non-invasive treatments (QWO, radiofrequency, ultrasound) is its durable, long-lasting results. However, it requires local anesthesia and small incisions, making it more invasive than pure non-invasive options. For patients seeking maximum cellulite improvement with long-term stability, Cellfina remains the gold standard treatment.
