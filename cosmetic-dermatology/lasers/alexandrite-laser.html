Understanding Alexandrite Laser: Comprehensive Overview

By DermoBrain Medical Editorial Team Updated 2026-03-06 Medically Reviewed ⏱ 3 min read

Alexandrite laser at 755 nm provides an optimal balance between melanin absorption and tissue penetration, making it the preferred choice for hair removal in lighter to moderate skin types (Fitzpatrick I-IV). This wavelength effectively targets melanin in hair follicles while maintaining safety and efficiency.

Technical Specifications

Alexandrite operates at 755 nm with high melanin absorption and moderate tissue penetration (3-4 mm). This optimal balance makes it highly efficient for hair removal in lighter skin types. Long-pulsed versions (3-20 milliseconds) used for hair removal with fluence 15-35 J/cm². Spot sizes typically 10-18 mm diameter. Dynamic cooling protects epidermis while delivering energy to hair follicles.

Key devices: Candela Gentlelase Plus, Cutera Solera, Cynosure Apogee Elite. These systems offer customizable parameters and rapid handpieces for efficient large-area treatment.

Hair Removal Applications

Gold standard for hair removal in Fitzpatrick I-IV skin types. Superior efficacy in lighter skin due to high melanin selectivity. Fluence 15-35 J/cm² depending on hair color and skin type. Large spot size (12-18 mm) enables rapid full-body treatment. Face: 20-25 minutes, legs: 60-90 minutes per leg, full body: 3-4 hours.

Treatment series: typically 6-8 sessions spaced 6-8 weeks apart for optimal hair reduction. Results: 80-95% permanent hair reduction after complete series. Fine hairs may require longer intervals or additional treatments. Maintenance treatments every 12-24 months preserve results.

Clinical Outcomes

Immediate hair reduction after first treatment. Progressive improvement with each session as dormant follicles enter growth phase and become amenable to treatment. Full results evident 3-6 months after final treatment. Patient satisfaction 85-95% for appropriate candidates. Fine or blonde hair shows less dramatic results than dark/coarse hair.

Skin Type Considerations

Optimal for Fitzpatrick I-IV. Use in type V-VI requires careful parameter adjustment and patch testing due to increased risk of post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation. Higher risk of textural changes or crusting in darker skin types. 810 nm or 1,064 nm better alternatives for V-VI.

Recovery and Side Effects

Transient erythema and edema 24-48 hours. Mild follicular edema (small bumps) 24-72 hours. Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation 5-15% in darker skin types, hypopigmentation <1%. Blistering rare (1-2%). Scarring very rare (<0.5%) with appropriate settings. Hair shedding 1-3 weeks post-treatment (expected, not regrowth).

Advantages and Limitations

Advantages: most efficient for light-moderate skin, rapid treatment time with large spots, excellent efficacy, predictable results. Limitations: less safe for darker skin types, less effective on fine/blonde hairs, requires frequent treatments for darker hair colors in lighter skin.

Safety Profile

Excellent safety record for appropriate skin types. Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation main side effect (5-15% in types III-IV, <5% in types I-II). Proper cooling and fluence selection critical to minimize complications. Absolute contraindication: recent sun exposure in darker skin types due to increased hyperpigmentation risk.

Conclusion

Alexandrite laser at 755 nm represents the gold standard for hair removal in Fitzpatrick I-IV skin types. High melanin selectivity combined with moderate tissue penetration enables safe, efficient, and highly effective treatment. Proper patient selection for skin type and careful parameter adjustment optimize outcomes and minimize complications.
