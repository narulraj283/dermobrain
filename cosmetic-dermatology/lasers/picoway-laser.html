PicoWay Laser: Versatile Picosecond Platform for Multiple Indications

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

PicoWay Technology Overview

PicoWay represents an advanced picosecond laser platform offering multiple wavelengths for comprehensive treatment of various skin concerns. Operating at 532-nanometer, 1,064-nanometer, and 1,320-nanometer wavelengths with picosecond pulse widths (290-370 picoseconds), PicoWay provides photoacoustic tissue interaction enabling efficient pigment removal and skin rejuvenation. The multi-wavelength platform allows treatment of diverse conditions with a single system.

PicoWay has become one of the most versatile laser platforms available, approved for tattoo removal, pigmented lesions, melasma, and fractional skin rejuvenation. The picosecond technology provides advantages over traditional nanosecond systems across all applications.

Multi-Wavelength Technology

PicoWay systems feature multiple wavelengths optimized for different indications:

  • 532-nanometer for red and orange pigments and vascular lesions
  • 1,064-nanometer for black and dark blue pigments
  • 1,320-nanometer for enhanced collagen stimulation and rejuvenation

The ability to switch wavelengths within a single treatment session allows efficient management of multicolor tattoos and complex pigmentation patterns.

Tattoo Removal Applications

PicoWay effectively removes professional and amateur tattoos of all colors. Black tattoos typically require 6-10 treatments, while colored tattoos vary based on ink characteristics. The multi-wavelength platform allows selection of optimal wavelength for each ink color, improving overall outcomes.

Pigmented Lesion and Melasma Treatment

The 1,064-nanometer wavelength effectively addresses solar lentigines, café-au-lait spots, and other benign pigmented lesions. For melasma treatment, the photoacoustic mechanism provides superior outcomes with reduced risk of post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation compared to thermal lasers. Some practitioners use fractionated approaches for enhanced collagen stimulation during melasma treatment.

Vascular and Redness Treatment

The 532-nanometer wavelength targets oxyhemoglobin in vascular lesions and persistent erythema. This wavelength option makes PicoWay suitable for treating rosacea, telangiectasia, and post-acne erythema. Multiple treatments at 4-6 week intervals typically produce optimal results.

Fractional Rejuvenation

Recent applications of PicoWay technology include fractional skin rejuvenation using the 1,320-nanometer wavelength. The fractional approach delivers energy in a pixelated pattern, stimulating collagen remodeling with minimal downtime. This application is particularly useful for combining pigment removal with concurrent skin rejuvenation in aging skin.

Treatment Parameters and Fluence

PicoWay allows precise control of treatment parameters based on indication. For tattoo removal, higher fluences create photoacoustic fragmentation. For pigmented lesion and melasma treatment, lower to moderate fluences minimize thermal injury. Spot size and pulse repetition rate can be adjusted to optimize treatment efficiency.

Results and Recovery

Immediate post-treatment effects include minimal erythema and occasional mild purpura. Minimal downtime is required, with patients returning to regular activities immediately. Progressive pigment clearance occurs over 4-8 weeks as cleared pigment is eliminated. Cumulative results improve with each treatment session.

Safety Across Skin Types

The photoacoustic mechanism and reduced thermal effect make PicoWay particularly safe in darker skin types. Risk of post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation and post-treatment scarring is minimized compared to older laser technologies. Appropriate fluence selection ensures safety across all Fitzpatrick skin types.
