Nasolabial Fold Fillers: Treatment of Smile Lines

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Nasolabial Fold Anatomy

The nasolabial fold is a natural line extending from the lateral ala of the nose to the lateral oral commissure. In youth, nasolabial folds are subtle and become prominent only during smiling. With age, these folds deepen and remain visible at rest due to descent of the medial cheek fat pad and decreased skin elasticity. Deep nasolabial folds create an aged appearance, and their softening is a primary goal in facial rejuvenation procedures.

Pathophysiology of Fold Deepening

Nasolabial fold deepening results from multiple factors: loss of suborbicularis oculi fat causing cheek hollowing, descent of the medial cheek tissue, loss of dermal collagen and elasticity, and habitual facial expressions. Gravity continuously pulls tissues downward throughout life, exacerbating this change. Sun exposure accelerates collagen breakdown. Genetic predisposition influences individual development of prominent nasolabial folds.

Filler Selection

Medium to thick HA fillers are appropriate for nasolabial fold treatment. Juvederm Ultra Plus XC and Restylane Lift provide adequate viscosity and lift. Radiesse offers extended duration and collagen stimulation benefits. The choice depends on fold depth, desired longevity, and patient factors. For moderate folds, lighter HA fillers suffice; deep folds benefit from thicker formulations or larger volumes.

Injection Technique

Effective nasolabial fold treatment requires deep filler placement along the periosteum of the pyriform aperture or within the periosteal plane. Superficial injection risks visible beading or lumps. Linear threading technique along the fold creates even distribution. Multiple small injections distributed along the fold's length prevent over-filling in single areas. Careful attention to symmetry ensures balanced appearance bilaterally.

Volume Considerations

Nasolabial fold treatment typically uses 0.5-1.5 mL per session, depending on fold depth and desired effect. Over-filling causes a puffy, unnatural appearance or an exaggerated smile line. Under-filling leaves residual fold visibility. Conservative initial treatment allows assessment of result and adjustment at follow-up if needed. Practitioners often treat in stages, allowing patient adaptation to enhanced appearance.

Combination with Cheek Filler

Nasolabial fold treatment is often combined with cheek augmentation, which addresses the underlying volume loss contributing to fold deepening. Supporting the cheek tissue with filler prevents fold descent and reduces need for aggressive fold filling. This combination approach provides superior, more natural-appearing results compared to isolated fold treatment.

Results and Timeline

Results are immediate; folds noticeably soften and volume appears restored within hours. Optimal appearance emerges at 2 weeks as swelling resolves. HA filler duration typically lasts 6-12 months depending on formulation and individual metabolism. Radiesse may last 12-18 months. Regular maintenance treatments every 6-12 months sustain desired appearance.

Special Considerations

The nasolabial fold is a prominent facial feature; results are highly visible. Patients should understand that subtle residual lines may persist, and perfecting this area often requires multiple modalities. Dynamic nasolabial folds during smiling are normal; treatment should not eliminate dynamic folding entirely but rather reduce static fold prominence.

Complications and Management

Hyaluronidase can dissolve HA fillers if asymmetry or overcorrection occurs. Bruising and swelling are common but resolve in 3-7 days. Nodules or lumps may result from superficial injection; these may be massaged or dissolved. Infection is rare but requires antibiotic treatment. Vascular complications are uncommon given the area's moderate vascularity.
