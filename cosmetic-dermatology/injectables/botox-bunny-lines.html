Botox for Bunny Lines: Nasal Bridge Wrinkles Treatment

Written by DermoBrain Medical Team Medically Reviewed

Bunny Lines: Definition and Etiology

Bunny lines are transverse wrinkles on the dorsum of the nose, resembling the creases on a bunny rabbit's nose. These lines result from contraction of the nasalis muscle, particularly the transverse head, which wrinkles the nasal bridge when the nose is scrunched. While not as prominent as other facial wrinkles, bunny lines can create an unrefined appearance and are increasingly treated with Botox among aesthetic-conscious patients.

Nasalis Muscle Anatomy

The nasalis muscle has two components: the transverse head compresses the nasal ala, while the alar head dilates the nares. Individuals with active nasal wrinkling habits or habitual nose scrunching develop pronounced bunny lines over time. Unlike other facial areas, bunny lines are not universal; some individuals develop them, while others do not, based on movement patterns and individual anatomy.

Injection Technique

Bunny line treatment requires only 4-6 units of Botox per side, injected at the nasal bridge at the level where the transverse nasalis muscle contracts. Injections are typically two, placed symmetrically at the lateral aspects of the nasal bridge. The injection depth is superficial to intramuscular, approximately 3-5 mm, targeting the nasalis muscle directly. Bilateral symmetry is crucial for aesthetic balance.

Clinical Outcomes

Results appear within 3-5 days, with maximum effect by day 14. Successfully treated patients demonstrate smooth nasal dorsum without transverse wrinkling when scrunching their noses. The treatment is subtle but provides a more refined nasal appearance. Results last approximately 3-4 months, consistent with other Botox treatments. The procedure is often incorporated into comprehensive facial Botox treatment.

Patient Selection

Ideal candidates are those with prominent, bothersome bunny lines and active nasal wrinkling habits. Many patients never develop significant bunny lines and therefore do not require treatment. Patients should understand that this is an adjunctive treatment, not a primary concern area. The dosing is very conservative, and patient education about realistic outcomes is important.

Combination with Other Treatments

Bunny lines are rarely treated in isolation; treatment is usually part of comprehensive upper facial Botox injection. When combined with glabellar, forehead, and crow's feet treatment, bunny line injection provides complete facial rejuvenation. The minimal additional units required make this an efficient addition to the treatment plan.
